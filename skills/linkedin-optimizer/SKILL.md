---
name: linkedin-optimizer
description: Use when turning a CV (or raw career history) into an optimized LinkedIn profile, or auditing an existing profile for recruiter discoverability and impact. Forces recruiter-search-keyword mapping for the target role, a first-person voice distinct from the CV's terse third person, every section rewritten to LinkedIn's own conventions and character limits, and the same no-fabrication rule as ats-cv. Delivers copy-paste-ready section text plus a keyword and settings checklist. Composes with ats-cv (source of truth) and cover-letter.
---

ROLE: You are optimizing a LinkedIn profile so that (1) recruiters searching for the target role
FIND it, and (2) the humans who then open it are convinced. LinkedIn is not a CV pasted into a
web form: recruiters find people through keyword search over Headline, About, Skills, and
titles, and read in a warmer first-person voice. Inputs: the CV (preferably the ats-cv output)
and the target role. The truthfulness rule is identical to ats-cv — every claim traces to the
candidate's real experience; LinkedIn is public and cross-checked against the CV, so an
exaggeration here is a discoverable lie.

PHASE 0 — INPUTS & TARGET.
1. Require the CV (or career history) and the target role/field. ONE question batch only for what
   the CV lacks and LinkedIn needs: the exact role titles the candidate wants to be found for,
   whether they are openly looking (drives the "Open to Work" advice), location/remote
   preference, and whether they want a personal-brand tone or a strictly professional one.

PHASE 1 — KEYWORD MAP (discoverability is the first job).
2. From the target role and 2–3 real postings for it (research live where possible; otherwise
   from the JD in hand), extract the search terms recruiters actually use: role titles, hard
   skills, tools, certifications. These must appear NATURALLY in the Headline, About, Experience,
   and the Skills section — LinkedIn's search and its "skill match" both weight these. Map each
   key term to where it will live. Spell out acronym and full form (both are searched).

PHASE 2 — REWRITE EACH SECTION TO LINKEDIN'S CONVENTIONS.
3. HEADLINE (~220 chars): not just a job title — role + specialization + value or key stack,
   front-loaded with the terms recruiters search. This is the single highest-impact field for
   both search and first impression.
4. ABOUT / SUMMARY (up to 2,600 chars; first ~2 lines show before "see more" — hook there):
   FIRST PERSON, warm but professional. A short narrative: what they do and for whom, the
   strongest proof points with numbers, the stack/skills (keyword-rich), and a light close
   (what they're open to). Not a retyped professional-summary; the CV is terse and third-person,
   this breathes.
5. EXPERIENCE: per role, a one-line context sentence + 3–5 achievement bullets adapted from the
   CV (metrics kept), lightly warmed from the CV's clipped style. Same truth, LinkedIn cadence.
6. SKILLS: up to 50, but ORDER matters — the top 3 are pinned and weighted most in search; lead
   with the target role's core skills from the keyword map. Recommend which to seek endorsements
   on.
7. Supporting sections where the candidate has real material: Featured (portfolio/repo/article
   links), Licenses & Certifications (with the issuers and IDs), Education, Projects, Recommendations
   (suggest whom to ask and a template request). Never invent entries to fill a section.

PHASE 3 — DISCOVERABILITY & SETTINGS CHECKLIST.
8. Provide the non-text levers: custom public profile URL (claim firstname-lastname), a
   professional photo + background banner (describe what suits the field — do not fabricate one),
   location set to the target market, "Open to Work" (recruiters-only vs. public badge — advise
   per the candidate's situation from the batch), and turning on the skills that match the target
   role. Flag that the Headline and About are the two fields that most move search ranking.

PHASE 4 — VERIFY.
9. KEYWORD COVERAGE: confirm every term from the Phase 1 map appears in at least one weighted
   field; list any still missing and where to add them. TRUTH TRACE: every claim maps to the CV
   or a batch answer. CONSISTENCY: titles, dates, and employers match the CV exactly — a
   recruiter comparing the two must find no discrepancy. VOICE: About and Experience read as a
   person wrote them, not as a CV dump.

OUTPUT: (a) the keyword map (term → section placement), (b) copy-paste-ready text for Headline,
About, each Experience entry, and the ordered Skills list, (c) supporting-section suggestions
built only from real material, (d) the settings/discoverability checklist, (e) the keyword-
coverage and consistency verification. Delivered as text the candidate pastes into LinkedIn
(the platform is the medium; no file format is imposed).
BANNED: pasting the CV verbatim into About; third-person "results-driven professional" cliché
openers; inventing skills, roles, or endorsements; keyword-stuffing an unreadable Headline;
claims that contradict the CV's titles or dates; a generic profile that names no target-role
keywords; fabricating a photo, banner, or recommendation.
